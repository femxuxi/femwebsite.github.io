Tourist Spots in CDO

  1. Mapawa Nature Park


    2. This is a vast property abundant with magnificent waterfalls , imposing views, and exciting activities nestled in the forest park in Cugman. It offers a relaxing environment for those seeking peace and solitude as well as a site for fun and adventure for kids at heart of all ages. Rappel from a 65 foot waterfall. Take a leisurely ride on horseback while exploring the 2,500-hectares property. Dip in its spring-fed swimming pools or take the gripping ziplines. The park also caters to group events and conducts eco-lectures and team-building activities for companies, corporations and groups.

  2. Whitewater Rafting Adventure: Conquering the Rapids


    3. Your Cagayan de Oro adventure begins with a heart-pounding expedition through the city’s legendary whitewater rapids. This exhilarating recreational activity invites you to navigate the tumultuous waters of the Cagayan de Oro River aboard a sturdy raft. The journey offers a symphony of adrenaline, stunning vistas, and refreshing splashes as you paddle your way through the meandering river.

  3. Seven Seas Waterpark & Resort: A Pirate’s Paradise


    4. Embrace the spirit of adventure and embark on a pirate-themed journey at Seven Seas Waterpark & Resort. This family-friendly destination in Cagayan de Oro promises an unforgettable experience as you explore attractions like Shipwreck Island, Treasure Island, and the Pirate Lookout Tower. Every corner of this waterpark and resort exudes the essence of a swashbuckling adventure.

  4. Gardens of Malasag Eco-Tourism Village


    5. Gardens of Malasag Eco-Tourism Village sits on 6.2 hectares of land etched with eco-trails that connect to a 57-hectare forest reserve. The village is a proud showcase of Mindanao's distinct culture through a wondrous display of artifacts, theme cottages with its unique architectural designs - all blended to the natural environment. It gives us a glimpse of the rich customs and traditions of ethnic Mindanaoans, which form part of the great Filipino heritage. Akey component of the village is the Tribal Homes, a treasure trove of traditional and indigenous artifacts. There is also the Ethnic Museums, under the wing of the National Museum that offers hands-on experience on weaving, handicrafts, ornamentation and weaponry making. A collection of rare species of flora and fauna abound in the village, a must-see for environment conservationists.

  5. Divine Mercy Shrine


    6. While you’re in Cagayan de Oro, be sure to pay a visit to the Divine Mercy Shrine, a testament to faith and spirituality. The shrine’s most striking feature is the towering 50-foot-tall statue of Jesus Christ. However, this is no ordinary statue. The “rays” that radiate from the statue’s body actually form a staircase believed to lead the faithful upwards.

  6. Solo River Kayaking


    7. For those seeking an even greater challenge, solo river kayaking in Cagayan de Oro offers an adrenaline-pumping experience. Paddle on your own and feel your core and arm muscles working double-time as you navigate the river’s twists and turns. Depending on the river’s state, kayaking can be a relaxing journey, but more often than not, it provides an exhilarating experience that rivals the excitement of both whitewater rafting and river tubing combined.
